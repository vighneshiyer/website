Jeera rice

  • Transfer rice with spices into pressure cooker, add water in a 2:1 water:rice ratio, and add a little salt
  • Pressure cook (in instant pot, about 4-5 minutes)

Home Fries

  • Quarter your potatoes, soak them in cold water
  • Place them on a plate and microwave for 4 minutes (covered) until they are almost tender
  • Cool the potatoes to room temperature
  • Cut them into uniform bite-size pieces
  • Melt butter in a steel pan, add some olive oil (use high heat)
  • Add onions, peppers, and potatoes when butter begins to brown
  • Season with salt + black pepper + cayenne + onion powder + garlic powder
  • Toss and cook until potatoes are crusty (10-12 minutes)
  • Tip: if you don’t want to use a microwave, you can also boil the potatoes until they are barely tender (don’t overcook potatoes since they will turn mushy and will fall apart)
    • You must precook the potatoes if you want to use onions/peppers and avoid overcooking them

Pesto alla Genovese (Ragusea)

  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gH3FgXRN4R8
  • 1 garlic clove per person
    • 2 parts pine nuts
    • 2 parts cheese (pecorino/parmesean)
    • 5 parts basil leaves
    • pinch of salt + olive oil
  • Knife method (chop garlic clove, nuts, cheese, leaves with rocking motion as fine as possible)
    • then use blade to grind mixture against board with coarse salt
    • mix in olive oil after transferring to bowl
    • texture is okay, but not as good as traditional
  • Mortar and pestle method (use granite or lava rock mortar - not ceramic - needs enough coarseness) (ceramic is suitable for spice powdering)
    • Mocajete is highly recommended for this
    • Grind garlic with salt (and some sugar)
    • Add raw pine nuts (toasted makes no difference to taste)
    • Chop basil leaves, then add, and grind against surface of mortar
    • Add grated pecorino + a little butter (traditional)
    • Switch to wooden spoon after done and add in oil, agitate and make the pesto creamy
  • Blanch basil leaves for 10 seconds and dump into ice water to prevent browning
    • this will slighly degrade the final flavor of the pesto

Pasta alla checca (uncooked tomatoes w/ pasta)

  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9hUdWu5oeb4
  • boil water, salt, and cook pasta (typically spaghetti) (can use a water kettle to speed up boiling process)
  • add lots of olive oil to a pan and set on low heat to warm up
    • add some sliced garlic if you’d like once the oil is hot, just to toast, but not brown
  • roughly cut heirloom and cherry tomatoes into cubes
  • add tomatoes to serving bowl directly, and dress with salt, and mix
  • mince fresh oregano and thyme (if using dried herbs, add them to the tomatoes directly so they can hydrate)
  • add a calabrian chilli (if you’d like)
  • save some pasta cooking water, and drain pasta
  • drain off water that has been leeched from the tomatoes
  • mix together tomatoes, chilli, and herbs, and rip basil into the mix too
  • add 10 cranks of black pepper
  • pour over hot olive oil from the pan directly onto the tomato mix
  • toss in pasta and mix everything together, add some pasta water to emulsify fat
    • mix aggressively in large bowl to form vinagrette
  • toss in fresh mozzarella torn up, and mix up to warm
  • serve with black pepper and grated parmesean
  • easy to store in fridge for reheating later

Minestrone

  • This is an aggregation of 4-5 different recipes; no amounts given, just use intuition - mostly based on Chef John’s recipe
  • Use a cast iron pot on medium heat
  • Steps
    • Chop vegetables (cabbage, spinach / kale / chard, tomatoes (fresh + canned San Marzano crushed), potatoes, zucchini, squash, sweet potatoes)
      • onion, celery, carrots, garlic - base
      • Add anything in the fridge that needs to be used up
      • No hot chillis / no paprika - this contaminates the flavor away from Italian (only pepper flakes if spice is desired)
    • Saute onion, celery, carrots with bay leaf in olive oil
    • Add spices (italian seasoning, oregano, basil, pepper flakes, garlic powder - only a small bit, rosemary, thyme, salt, black pepper, bay leaves) and toast
    • Add tomatoes (crushed) and saute for a few minutes to deglaze
    • Add other vegetables and saute
    • Add a rind of parmesean cheese to the pot (protip)
    • Add water (or vegetable broth / vegetable boullion cube) and cover to boil and cook
    • Add precooked beans (white beans, navy beans, cannelini beans, lima beans, kidney beans, garbanzo beans) near the end of vegetables being done
    • Add greens (kale / spinach / chard) as you’re almost done
    • Adjust salt and pepper
    • Add pasta (10 minutes before done) and cook through
      • Often better to forgo this step and add pasta just when you’re about to consume the soup (reboil for 10 minutes)
      • Red lentil pasta is best for retaining shape and texture
    • When serving, finish with a large amount of microplaned parmesean cheese and olive oil

Bhajis / Vegetable Curries

Green Beans Bhaji

  • Temper mustard seeds, hing, cumin seeds, and dried chillis in coconut oil, then add freshly cut green chilli
  • Add chopped onion, saute for 2 minutes and then add turmeric
  • Add green beans and carrots (cut into small pieces, but not too small)
  • Cook covered with salt + sugar + a bit of water for 20 minutes to fully cook beans (optionally add some cumin and corriander powder)
  • Add shredded coconut and cook until incorporated

Pav Bhaji

  • Pressure cook vegetables (cauliflower, potatoes, carrots, capcisum, chillis, peas) until mushy
  • In a tava, add butter, and fry off cumin seeds and onions until golden
  • Add ginger / garlic paste + turmeric + green chilli
  • Add tomatoes and water, begin mashing
  • Add spices (corriander powder, fennel powder, amchur powder, dried fenugreek leaves, red chilli powder, hing, salt)
  • Add vegetables from pressure cooker and mash mixture
  • Add butter, pav bhaji masala, adjust salt levels
  • Add chopped cilantro, serve with onion and lemon juice

Eggplant Roast

  • For a gluten free South Indian “breading” alternative:
    • add 1 part urad dal, 2 parts rice, 0.5 parts methi (fenugreek) seeds, 1 dried red chilli, and a pinch of hing to a blender
    • blend to a medium fine powder, use as breading for eggplant
  • Add coconut oil to a cast iron pan
  • Once hot, add mustard seeds, hing powder, red chilli powder, turmeric, dried red chilli
  • Once crackling, toss in eggplant (cut into thick wedges) and salt
  • Slowly roast over several hours using low heat, keep turning over
    • Initially cover with lid for a few minutes at a time to fully cook the eggplant before it darkens
    • Scrape bottom of pan regularly, add more oil as needed
    • Add the breading mix a little at a time over 30 minutes
  • Finish with amchur powder and/or lemon juice and adjust salt

Shahi Cauliflower / Paneer

  • Saute cut cauliflower in ghee with salt + turmeric + one green chilli cut in half until just under fully cooked (or roast in oven)
  • To make the shahi gravy
    • Heat oil then add, geera seeds, fennel seeds, onion / garlic / ginger paste, salt, turmeric, red chilli powder
    • Then add tomatoes (deseeded) and tomato paste, soaked almonds, and frozen shredded coconut
    • Finish with a few mint leaves (raw), then transfer to a blender
    • Add 3-4 cardamom pods (seeds only) to the blender and blend to form a thick gravy
  • Put it together
    • In a pot, heat oil, then add a bay leaf, some cloves, geera seeds, red chilli powder
    • Once crackling, add blended gravy and water down
    • Add corriander and cumin powder, garam masala / kitchen king masala, and adjust salt
    • Cover and cook to thicken for 5 minutes on low heat
    • Add the cooked cauliflower and some more water to fully cook the cauliflower
    • Let cauliflower fully cook covered on medium-high heat, then add dried fenugreek leaves
    • Finish with cilantro before serving

Chana Masala

  • Soak 1 cup of dried chickpeas for 4 hours (or used canned chickpeas)
  • In a pan, saute cumin seeds, bay leaves, black pepper, cinnamon over high heat in ghee (optional cardamom, cloves)
  • Add diced onions and saute for another 3 minutes (until browned)
  • Add ginger / garlic / green chilli paste in 30 seconds and saute until the raw edge is gone + turmeric
  • Add fresh diced tomatoes and tomato paste and deglaze
  • Add spices (turmeric, cumin powder, corriander powder, garam masala, chana masala powder) and roast until aromatic
  • Add cooked chickpeas (or add soaked chickpeas and pressure cook in instant pot for 35 minutes, use 20 minute natural pressure release)
  • After cooking, add fenugreek leaves and amchur (dried mango) powder
  • Finish with lime juice and chopped cilantro (and optionally some honey / sugar)

Khichidi (Instant Pot) (Lentils and Rice)

  • Prep
    • Soak 1 cup dal (moong / toor / masoor) and 1/2 - 1 cup rice (jasmine) for 30 minutes
    • Prepare a few (3-4) cups of vegetables (peas, carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, spinach, beans, cabbage, cauliflower) in bite-size chunks
    • Prepare a garlic / ginger / green chilli paste with 1:1:0.5 ratio
    • Chop an onion (diced) - optional
  • Steps
    • Heat pot on medium-high heat, add ghee, add cumin seeds when hot
    • Then add hing, peppercorns, curry leaves (optional), bay leaf
    • Once aromatic (30 seconds), add garlic / ginger / chilli paste and saute for 30 seconds + onion (optional)
    • Add turmeric, cumin powder, chilli powder, and salt
    • Add chopped vegetables and saute for a few minutes
    • Add pre-soaked rice + dal + many cups of water (1 cup dal + 4 cups vegetables = 6 cups water, adjust accordingly)
    • Pressure cook for 15 minutes
    • After done, turn off IP, and let pressure release naturally over 20 minutes before pulling the pressure valve manually
    • Add water or continue to boil to adjust consistency

Yellow Dal (Nose Style)

  • Steps
    • Pressure cook masoor/toor dal
    • Fry cumin seeds and asafoetida in ghee
    • Add garlic/ginger (ideally as a paste) and finely diced onions and 1 green chilli
    • When golden, add spices (cumin powder, coriander powder (1:2 ratio), garam masala, salt, turmeric, black pepper)
    • When spices are aromatic, add diced tomatoes and use tomato water to deglaze the pan
    • Add peanuts and water and cover to cook for 5 minutes
    • When you have formed a thick slushy paste, add cooked dal
    • Let come to a boil, then finish with dried fenugreek leaves and sugar to taste (or honey or coconut sugar)
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves

    • too much sugar added, next time only 1.5x spoons
    • too few tomatoes added (only 2-3x reg size romas) + cut them into smaller pieces
    • add more water during cooking and after its done
    • no garam masala, only corriander and geera ground spices
    • add peanuts earlier in the cooking process so they cook fully, mine were a little raw
    • more notes
      • Use generous heat, cut everything beforehand, don’t overcook onions, no garlic, only use geera seeds, use corriander/geera powder and turmeric only
      • Add sugar and cilantro at end

      • Add enough water to keep thin

Dal Makhani

  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MUesLbKX1bA
  • Chop lots of ginger with skin on into coarse pieces (rounds), and chop green chilli lengthwise into halves
  • Soak urad dal and kidney beans overnight
  • In an empty pot, add soaked kidney beans and dal, cover with water +1 inch, add ginger pieces and chilli halves, and add pinch of salt
  • Cook for 90 minutes to at least partially cook dal, bring to boil
  • In separate pot, make the masala.
    • Chop 4x tomatoes, and 1x yellow onion
    • Bring oil to temperature, add cumin seeds, add onions, corriander powder, red chilli powder, then tomatoes quickly (can add other spices here too)
    • When onions and tomatoes disentegrate, pour in lentils from other pot
    • Then cook additional 30-45 minutes
  • Bring heat down, and add butter pieces and 200ml of heavy cream, and then garam masala (since it’s already cooked)
  • Finally add fenugreek leaves at the end as a powder, adjust for salt. Done

Dal Makhani (mother’s recipe)

Dal Makhani (Honest Kitchen variant)

  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WE2wpEvIddg
  1. Wash + soak 1/2 cup whole urad dal overnight
  2. Wash + soak 1/4 cup split urad dal overnight
  3. Boil whole urad with only a small pinch of salt for 20 mins then drain the dal. The dal should have split.
  4. Add whole and split urad to pot with ginger/garlic paste and tomato puree and kasmiri chili powder and some more salt.
  5. Cook in pressure cooker for many whistles
  6. Keep cooking in the same pot until the dal texture has thickened

Sambar

  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JW7Y8WHd07s
  • Equal parts masoor and toor dal and pressure cook until soft and done, add salt to dal once cooked to taste
  • For masala, add oil, add mustard seeds when oil is hot, then add garlic and ginger, green chilli, turmeric powder
  • Then add onion and tomatoes after 1 minute, cook lightly until soft (on lower heat)
  • Add to pre-cooked dal, then add fresh cilantro

Sambar (another variant)

  • Roast chana dal, urad dal, mustard seeds, chillis, curry leaves, and hing in coconut oil/ghee
  • Then add vegetables (butternut squash, carrots, eggplant, etc., except tomato) and cook for 5 minutes
  • Add tomatoes + salt + turmeric powder + cook for 2 mins
  • Adjust spices (turmeric powder, cumin powder, black pepper, cayenne powder)
  • Add tamarind juice (or lime juice) + water + boil for 10-15 minutes to fully cook all the vegetables
  • Add pre-cooked dal (masoor/toor) and water and bring to a boil
  • Add sambar powder, and boil additional 5 minutes
  • Finally turn off heat, add cilantro, salt, and sugar/jaggery
  • Critical point: don’t add too many vegetables, only an equal amount to the dal or the mix will be too dilute and the vegetables will take too long to cook (I’ve made this mistake twice now, trying to add some squash, carrots x4, bell pepper x1, onion) just stick to less than 1/2 of the amount

Adai (Lentil Crepe)

  • Soak 1 cup of rice, 1/4 cup chana dal, 1/4 cup toor dal (for 3 hours)
  • Soak 2 tbsp of urad dal (traditionally the whole urad is used, but the skinless version is also OK) (for 8+ hours)
  • Rinse all dals and rice and add to a grinder
    • Add spices (dried Kasmiri red chillis, salt, hing, turmeric powder, fresh ginger, cumin seeds, cumin powder, curry leaves) to grinder
    • Grind until thick batter
  • Taste ground mixture and adjust salt levels, add some ground pepper
  • Rest for 2 hours in fridge
  • Fry adais on cast iron plate with coconut oil (like pancakes)

SCD (Gluten Free) Naan

  • Ingredients
    • 1 egg
    • 1/4 tsp baking soda
    • 1/3 cup coconut milk (or almond milk or water)
    • 1 1/2 tsp olive oil
    • 1 cup almond flour
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • garlic and cilantro finely chopped
  • Steps
    • Whisk egg, soda, coconut milk, and olive oil until frothy
    • When ready to fry the bread, mix the almond flour and salt with the wet mix
    • Pour into hot oiled pan and cook like pancakes
    • Top with garlic and cilantro

Gluten Free Banana Bread

  • Ingredients
    • Base recipe
      • 1.5 - 2 cups gluten-free 1:1 flour
      • 4-5 bananas
      • 1/2 cup sugar/honey
      • 1 tsp baking soda
      • 1.5 tsp baking powder
      • 2 large eggs
      • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
      • splash of milk (2 tbsp)
    • Spices
      • 1 tsp cinnamon (can add more)
      • 1/4 tsp freshly ground nutmeg
      • 1/4 tsp ginger powder
      • splash of vanilla extract
      • 1/4 cup crushed walnuts
      • 1/4 tsp salt (optional)
  • Steps
    • Mix dry (spices + flour + soda + sugar) and wet (bananas, eggs, milk, butter) ingredients separately
    • Mix dry batch into wet batch and whisk until ‘batter’ consistency (similar to pancakes)
    • Bake in bread pan at 350 F for 50-60 minutes (err on the longer side)
    • Cool in pan for 15 minutes, then cool on wire rack for 30 minutes

Gluten Free Carrot Cake

  • Dry ingredients
    • 2 - 2.5 cups GF 1:1 flour
    • 1 1/4 tsp baking powder
    • 1 tsp baking soda
    • 2-3 tsp cinnamon (can add more)
    • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
    • 1/8 tsp clove
    • 1/4 tsp allspice
    • 1/2 tsp salt (optional)
    • 1.5 cups sugar (can add more for sufficient sweetness, supplement with honey if needed)
    • 1/2 - 1 cup walnuts or pecans
  • Wet ingredients
    • 3 or 4 eggs
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
    • 2/3 - 1 cup oil (coconut oil, or substitute with equivalent amount of butter)
    • 1/3 cup applesauce
    • 3 cups shredded carrots (5-6 carrots or 1 lbs)
    • 1/2 cup buttermilk (add less than full amount if using 1 cup of oil)
    • 1/2 - 1 cup coconut flakes
    • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Combine wet and dry until it forms a smooth batter
  • Pour into bread pan and bake at 350 F for 35-40 minutes (going up to 50 minutes)
  • Check with toothpick to see if baked through

Gluten Free Sandwich Bread (Julia Child’s Style)

    • 2 1/2 cups warm water

    • 2 tsp salt
    • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • Can cut recipe in 1/3 for a small loaf
  • Steps
    • Pour 1/2 cup water + yeast + sugar (after heating the water to near 100 F) - let sit in warm place for 5 minutes to bloom yeast
    • Add rest of water + 1/2 of flour in a mixing bowl - stir well
    • Add rest of flour + salt + melted butter - stir and knead
    • Shape into ball, place into the bowl, and cover and let proof for 1 - 1 1/2 hours
    • Using a 9 x 5 loaf pan, butter the pans, and place the dough into the pan after folding it in thirds (place seam side down)
    • Let rise for 1 hour in loaf pan while covering it with tea towel
    • Preheat oven to 375 F
    • Brush loaf with milk, and bake for 30-35 minutes
